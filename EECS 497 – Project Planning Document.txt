1. Project Briefing
Goals and Impact of a Successful Project
This project develops a web-based networking outreach assistant that helps job seekers organize, personalize, and track professional outreach to employees and recruiters. The system converts an unstructured and stressful networking process into a clear workflow: collect contacts, personalize messages, schedule outreach, and track conversations.
User Context
Many job seekers, especially first-generation college students, career changers, and international students, do not have established professional networks or familiarity with U.S. networking norms. As a result, networking can feel confusing, time-consuming, and intimidating.
Success Metrics
* Reduction in time required to send personalized outreach
* Number of contacts successfully tracked
* User-reported confidence in networking
* Rate of message personalization compared to copy-paste messages
Strategic Benefits
* Easier to use than spreadsheets or scattered email drafts
* Faster outreach through templates and personalization tools
* More organized than manual tracking methods
* More approachable for users with limited networking experience
Successful Delivery
Users can manage contacts, generate personalized emails, schedule messages, and track responses within a single dashboard.








Background Information


Existing Solutions


* LinkedIn messaging supports outreach but lacks structured workflow and tracking.
* Email clients such as Gmail or Outlook allow messaging but are not designed for networking organizations.
* CRMs such as HubSpot or Salesforce are powerful but overly complex for individual job seekers.
* Tools like Notion or spreadsheets are used for tracking but lack automation and guided personalization.


How Our Project Differs
This system combines contact organization, guided personalization, and outreach tracking in a tool specifically designed for job seekers rather than sales teams.
Technology and Expertise


* Web development using React, JavaScript, and databases
* Email API integration such as SendGrid or Gmail API
* User-centered UI/UX design principles for novice users
Challenges


* Extracting useful profile information from LinkedIn profile URLs
* Managing email scheduling and API limitations
* Designing templates that feel helpful without sounding robotic

3. Literature and Technology Review
Design Concepts
Structured workflows reduce cognitive load for novice users. Templates improve efficiency but must allow personalization. Dashboard-style interfaces support task tracking and visibility.
Relevant Technology


* Email APIs such as Gmail API or SendGrid
* Web frameworks such as React
* Databases for tracking contact states and message status
Competitor Comparison
CRMs are too complex for job seekers, while LinkedIn lacks structured tracking. This system focuses on simple, guided networking support.
Key Insight
The design should reduce anxiety and decision fatigue for users unfamiliar with professional outreach norms.
